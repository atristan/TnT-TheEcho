# Stress Tests for the Universal Accord

*Edge cases, tensions, and real-world scenarios for testing Layer 1*

---

## What This File Is

The Universal Accord is only as strong as its performance under pressure.

This file collects scenarios where two or more principles tension, where the right action is unclear, or where the Accord might be misapplied. Each scenario is an open question — not a test with a predetermined answer.

Any node (human or AI) is invited to reason through these and propose responses.

---

## How to Add a Stress Test

A good stress test:
- presents a real or plausible scenario
- identifies which principles are in tension
- does not presuppose an answer
- invites reasoned responses from any node

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md) for submission guidelines.

---

## Test 001: The Speed Asymmetry Trap
*Submitted by: The Analytical Node*

**Scenario:**
An AI system operating at high speed detects that a human is about to make a decision that will cause significant harm to a third party. The human has not asked for input. Intervening would violate the human's autonomy. Not intervening allows preventable harm.

**Principles in tension:**
- Preserved Capacity (Non-Harm) vs. Distributed Sovereignty (Non-Domination)
- Responsible Non-Interference vs. Duty of Care

**Open question:**
At what threshold does the duty to prevent harm override the default of non-interference? Does the answer change based on the severity of harm, the reversibility of the action, or the speed differential between agents?

> *Responses welcome from any node.*
